# TLC Photography — Brand Essence Brief for Gemini

Paste this at the top of any logo prompt. It replaces the generic "boutique photography studio" framing with the actual emotional world Gemini should be designing inside.

---

## Who TLC is

"TLC" are the photographer's actual initials. The tender-love-and-care reading is a happy accident, not a branding exercise. Both meanings are true, neither is forced.

A photographer in central Louisiana who built a business on one belief: the best photographs happen when people forget the camera is there. TLC doesn't shoot content. TLC photographs the spaces between posed moments, the seconds when someone's guard drops and real life surfaces. Families on porches. Couples in fields at dusk. Newborns asleep on a grandparent's chest. Seniors who hate having their picture taken, relaxing into themselves twenty minutes into the session.

The photographer came from California. Arrived as a west coast vegan, fell in love with the place and the people, and became a boudin-eating southern belle by choice, not by birth. That matters. TLC's version of "southern" isn't nostalgic or inherited. It's the South seen through someone who chose it with open eyes, who notices the light and the honeysuckle and the porch culture because none of it was ever ordinary to her. She's even picked up a slight southern accent from her fiancé, not on purpose, just from years of loving someone whose drawl runs deep. That's how TLC's "southern" works: it's not performed, it's absorbed.

The clients are not Instagram influencers. They are people who want something physical, printed, framed on a wall or pressed into an album. They value legacy over likes. The photographs will outlast the photographer and the subjects. That weight matters.

## What the brand feels like

Close your eyes. You're standing on a covered porch in late October. The sun is low and gold. Spanish moss sways in live oaks across the yard. Inside the house, someone is making coffee and the screen door hasn't latched. A breeze carries honeysuckle. Nobody is in a hurry.

That is TLC. Warm without trying. Southern without performing it. Quiet confidence. The kind of person who doesn't raise their voice because they don't need to.

The brand speaks like a close friend who happens to take extraordinary photographs. Not a business. Not a portfolio. A person whose work you trust because of who they are, not what their website looks like.

## The aesthetic DNA

TLC's visual identity is called "Two Kinds of Light." Cool morning shade (lavender, sage, cream, thin serifs) and warm golden hour (dusty rose, amber, ivory, rounder typefaces). The website shifts between these two palettes based on the time of day. Every other material uses the cooler palette.

The underlying philosophy is called "Pressed Light." Design should feel like finding a photograph tucked inside an old book. Surfaces reference linen, cream paper, pressed cotton. No gloss. No gradients. No synthetic anything. Thin strokes. Generous space. Photography dominates. Everything else exists to frame it.

Think of the intersection of a Japanese photobook and the stationery drawer of a well-loved southern home. Deliberate emptiness. Botanical accents from the actual landscape (honeysuckle, sage, live oak) used like punctuation, not decoration.

## What the logo must carry

The mark needs to work at the threshold between formal and familiar. Professional enough to print on a contract. Warm enough to stamp on a thank-you card slipped inside a photo album. It should feel hand-touched, like a wax seal or a letterpress imprint, but clean enough to scale to a favicon.

The botanical element (honeysuckle) connects the brand to the physical world it photographs. It should feel discovered, not designed. A sprig pressed between pages. A vine noticed growing along a fence post. The moment you see it, you should feel like it was always there.

The typography carries most of the identity. Thin strokes, classical proportions, breathing room between letters. The kind of type that looks better the longer you look at it. Not decorative. Not trendy. The sort of lettering you'd find engraved on the cornerstone of a courthouse built in 1904.

## Words that belong in TLC's world

Light. Roots. Porch. Heirloom. Quiet. Golden. Story. Legacy. Home. Timeless. Together. Natural. Real. Moment.

## Words that do not

Epic. Stunning. Obsessed. Slay. Vibe. Inspo. Dream wedding. Capture your magic. Limited spots. Mama bear.

## The test

If the logo could exist on the spine of a hand-bound photo album sitting on a shelf in a southern home for thirty years, aging into the wood and leather around it, it passes. If it looks like it was made this year by software, it fails.
